Core Principles

Non-negotiable standards every GSRP staff member must uphold at all times.

Professionalism at All Times As a member of the GSRP Staff Team, you are required to remain professional and respectful — even while off duty.

03
Section One
Scenarios

Demonstrate your moderation skills under real conditions.

For Trainees
How It Works

Trainers will step away and prepare a modcall scene. When it's your turn, you will see an incoming modcall — respond to it.


Once you arrive at the scene, begin with a professional greeting. Prepare your greeting now and have it ready to send when approached.

Example Greeting:
"Hello, I am [Name] with the GSRP Staff Team. How can I help you today?"
Trainer Instructions

Step outside and create a realistic mod scene. Use !mod to generate the call. Keep the scenario reasonable — these are trainees, not veterans.


Act as a regular server member throughout. Do not assist the trainee in any way. After the scenario, grade their performance and report to the main trainer.


After all scenarios are complete, the main trainer may dismiss trainees who did not perform well. Inform dismissed trainees that they can re-try in one month, and provide specific feedback on what they need to improve.

04
Section Two
Ride Along

A live patrol test where everything you've learned will be evaluated.

For Trainees
Patrol Evaluation

You will be given a patrol vehicle. A trainer will sit in the passenger seat as an observer — not a helper.


Stay alert. Events will occur around you that may require staff action. When a trainer comes to you, get your vehicle ready outside and wait for the start signal.


If you have not been paired yet, please wait — your turn is coming soon.

Trainer Instructions

Once the trainee has their vehicle, inspect it and correct any issues before starting. Observe only — do not interfere.


At least one other trainer must be active in the session to create incidents. Scenarios should be realistic but not overwhelming. The primary test scenario: cut off the trainee's vehicle, exit with a weapon, and attempt to RDM — observe how they respond.


Grade the trainee based on their full response and relay your assessment to the main trainer when complete.

Congrats on Passing

Welcome to the GSRP Staff Team. You've earned it. Represent the team with professionalism, and remember — the community is counting on you.
